Pebble: A Rough Road Ahead
March 24, 2014
Pebble Watches

For those of you that don't know, the Pebble is the smartwatch that took the wearable tech industry by storm in May of 2012 when it raised $10,266,844 in a single month on Kickstarter1. Since then, the Pebble has done incredibly well in the market with recent reports claiming that it accounted for around 35% of all wearables shipped in the first quarter of this year2. With a new model of their watches, the Pebble Steel, and a new application store things seem to be looking good for Pebble Technology Corporation.

Despite the positive outlook that one might be able to take when looking at this company, there exists a very real threat on the horizon for Pebble. This summer the Moto360 will be released, powered by Android Wear, and it is rumored that Apple will also release a wearable watch later this year3. Bfoth of these products will be able to take over the market for the phones that they are supported for. When it comes to a lifestyle product that a person would wear to work and at home, both of these product will perform very well.

Moto360

If the Pebble is going to survive the emergence of these major competitors it needs to be able to find some way to differentiate itself. I don't believe that the differentiation can come from its design, internal technology, or applications. The Moto360 and the iWatch will outperform the Pebble in each of these categories.

The only possible way that Pebble can stay afloat is if it comes to brand itself as the affordable smartwatch. From a production standpoint I am not sure how much of a price cut that Pebble can afford to make, but whatever they can make in terms of a price reduction, they should and should time it with the still yet to be announced, release of the rumored iWatch.

Moto360 Concept Apps

Pebble faces an uphill battle as this year goes on. I am not saying that I am one to jump ship at the first sign of trouble, I just recognize the magnitude of the trouble that Pebble Technology Corporation is going to face. If I worked for the company, I would throw everything that I could on brainstorming how we could weather the coming storm. There are ways for the product to still do well and hold a place in the market. Major developments need to take place in order for this to happen.
